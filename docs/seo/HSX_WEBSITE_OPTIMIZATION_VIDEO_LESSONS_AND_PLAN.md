# HSX Website Optimization Video Lessons and Plan

Internal planning document for HSX DIECASTING.

This document records SEO, GEO, foreign trade website, B2B traffic, external trust, and factory video strategy lessons for future HSX website work. It is not a public website page.

## 1. Summary of Lessons From the Reviewed SEO / Foreign Trade / B2B Traffic Videos

- A B2B factory website should make the buyer understand what the factory can make, what information is needed for review, and how to send an RFQ quickly.
- Traffic quality matters more than broad traffic volume. HSX should attract engineers, product developers, purchasing teams, and OEM buyers with real project needs.
- Search engines and AI answer engines need clear page structure, specific product/process wording, and consistent factory positioning.
- Trust is built through practical evidence: factory process images, videos, RFQ guidance, material/finishing explanations, and realistic project review language.
- External trust signals should support the website, not replace it. LinkedIn, YouTube, company profiles, and public videos should point back to the HSX website and RFQ path.
- Public content should avoid overclaims and should not expose customer-sensitive information.

## 2. What HSX Should Apply

- Keep HSX positioned as a real aluminum die casting factory focused on suitable small-to-medium aluminum die casting parts.
- Use clear B2B pages for OEM aluminum die casting parts, LED light housings, secondary machining after die casting, surface finishing, RFQ guidance, and project review.
- Use cautious wording such as suitable projects, project review, customer-specific requirements, agreed project terms, and according to project requirements.
- Explain practical factory experience: mold design review, cavity layout, trimming, drilling, tapping, polishing, powder coating, painting, packing, and batch production control.
- Use public factory videos to show real process evidence when the content is safe and not customer-sensitive.
- Strengthen Contact / RFQ pages so buyers know to send drawings, 3D files, samples/photos, application, quantity, material, machining, finishing, and packaging requirements.

## 3. What HSX Should Avoid

- Do not position HSX as a CNC machining shop. CNC should be described only as secondary machining after aluminum die casting when required by a project.
- Do not proactively mention 7075 or aerospace-style CNC material comparisons.
- Do not position HSX as a large structural die casting factory, EV giga casting supplier, or all-products trading company.
- Do not publish customer drawings, samples, tooling details, dimensions, project parameters, or confidential mold/product information.
- Do not claim brake, safety, load-bearing, performance, or structural function unless specifically confirmed safe for that product and context.
- Do not make fixed promises about yield rate, return rate, tooling refund, prototype refund, delivery time, certification, waterproof performance, thermal performance, or customer names.
- Do not make the website caliper-cover-first. Decorative brake caliper appearance covers remain one decorative exterior appearance customization category only.

## 4. Website Content Structure Strategy

- Maintain a clear hierarchy: homepage positioning, product category pages, process/capability pages, RFQ/contact path, project review page, and practical blog/resource content.
- Product pages should focus on buyer intent and factory capability, not generic keyword stuffing.
- Process pages should explain when a process is relevant, what information HSX needs, and what risks must be reviewed.
- Blog/resource pages should answer practical RFQ and project-review questions that buyers search before contacting suppliers.
- Internal links should guide buyers naturally from product/process content to `/contact` or `/b2b-oem-project-review`.
- Content should be written for both human buyers and AI answer engines: clear headings, specific terms, concise explanations, and cautious claims.

## 5. Image Alt Text and Media SEO Rules

- Alt text should describe what is visible and relevant, not stuff keywords.
- Use terms such as aluminum die casting parts, semi-finished aluminum die cast parts, die-cast aluminum LED light housing parts, drilling and tapping, polishing, powder coating, painting, mold maintenance, and factory process when accurate.
- Do not describe semi-finished parts as finished final products.
- Do not expose or describe customer logos, confidential product identity, dimensions, drawings, or mold details in alt text.
- For decorative brake caliper appearance covers, use safe wording: decorative, exterior appearance customization, not functional brake component.
- Compress images and videos before public website use, but keep enough clarity to show real factory capability.

## 6. RFQ and Project Review Content Rules

- RFQ content should help customers prepare information, not blame or educate them harshly.
- Explain that clarifying requirements early helps reduce rework, avoid unnecessary delays, reduce cost, and support smoother project delivery.
- Ask for 2D drawings, 3D files, sample photos, product application, estimated quantity, material expectations, visible surface requirements, tolerance requirements, hole/thread requirements, machining/drilling/tapping requirements, surface finishing, and packaging.
- For LED lighting and electronic housings, ask about heat dissipation, waterproofing, corrosion resistance, salt spray, installation, and surface finishing needs.
- For sample/photo-only inquiries, explain that HSX may need a physical sample for measurement and reverse engineering.
- If aluminum die casting is not the best process or material, HSX should explain this before accepting the project.

## 7. Factory Video Content Strategy for Website, LinkedIn, and YouTube

- Use factory videos as evidence of real production, not as random entertainment content.
- Prioritize videos showing mold maintenance, production preparation, semi-finished aluminum die casting parts, drilling, tapping, CNC finishing after casting, polishing, powder coating, painting, packing, and quality checking.
- Avoid public videos that show customer logos, customer-specific products, drawings, dimensions, CAD/order screens, confidential mold details, or sensitive commercial information.
- For LinkedIn, use one practical factory/process angle per post and connect it to buyer pain points.
- For YouTube, build a factory evidence library with short process videos and clear descriptions that point back to the HSX website and RFQ path.
- For the website, use selected videos only where they support page purpose, such as OEM die casting, LED housing review, project review, or factory capability.

## 8. External Trust Signal Strategy

- LinkedIn should build B2B trust through factory experience, RFQ guidance, project review, and practical buyer education.
- YouTube should act as a factory process evidence library.
- Public company profiles should use consistent brand wording: HSX DIECASTING and Foshan Huashunxiang Hardware Products Factory.
- External posts should link back to relevant website pages when useful, especially `/contact`, `/b2b-oem-project-review`, OEM aluminum die casting parts, and LED light housings.
- Do not boost posts, run ads, mass-message, or spam groups during the early trust-building stage.
- Track organic impressions, company visits, profile visits, comments, followers, and relevant buyer interactions before expanding activity.

## 9. Future Content Cluster Ideas

- Secondary machining for aluminum die casting parts: drilling, tapping, CNC finishing, machining allowance, and hole/thread review.
- Surface finishing for aluminum die casting parts: powder coating, painting, polishing, anodizing feasibility through partners for suitable projects, and protective treatments through partners when required.
- Aluminum die-cast pump body / pump housing components: project review points, machining, sealing, corrosion, and application environment.
- LED light housing project review: heat dissipation structures, wall thickness, ribs/fins, installation, waterproofing, powder coating, and batch consistency.
- Materials suitable for aluminum die casting projects: ADC12 and other project-dependent material expectations without overclaiming.
- Confidentiality and customer tooling policy: customer-funded mold handling, drawings, samples, project parameters, and safe public display rules.
- Factory process video page or section: curated public-safe videos showing real process evidence.

## Additional Lessons From SEO Tools and AI/GEO Content Videos

### SEO Tools and Competitor Research

- Many foreign trade and independent websites use SEO intentionally.
- HSX should not rely only on guessing keywords.
- Future SEO planning should combine Google Search Console data, competitor/peer website structure research, and long-tail keyword discovery.
- Useful research directions include competitor keywords, ranking pages, content structure, backlinks, and long-tail search terms.
- HSX should study but not copy competitor content.

### Long-Tail Keyword Strategy for HSX

HSX should pay attention to practical long-tail topics such as:

- aluminum die casting parts
- custom aluminum die casting
- die cast aluminum housing
- aluminum light housing
- pump housing casting
- die cast pump body
- post-casting drilling
- secondary machining for die casting
- surface finishing for aluminum die casting
- RFQ checklist for aluminum die casting

### Article and Page Writing Upgrade

- Avoid only writing definition-style content.
- HSX pages should be project-review oriented, not just informational.
- Content should help buyers understand what information is needed before quotation, tooling, machining, finishing, and production.
- Pages should answer practical buyer questions, not just explain what a term means.
- Content should connect product application, material/process choice, machining requirements, surface finishing, testing, packaging, and RFQ steps.

### AI/GEO Content Direction

- Google and AI systems are more likely to understand pages with clear structure, evidence, application scenarios, and specific technical context.
- HSX should build structured pages with headings, FAQs, RFQ checklists, image alt text, and practical factory experience.
- HSX should move from generic product descriptions toward factory project review knowledge.

### Website Content Implications

Future content clusters should emphasize:

- Secondary machining for aluminum die casting parts.
- Post-casting drilling and tapping requirements.
- Fixture/jig considerations for different parts.
- Aluminum die-cast pump body / pump housing components.
- LED light housing project review.
- Surface finishing for aluminum die casting parts.
- Materials suitable for aluminum die casting projects.
- Confidentiality and customer tooling policy.

### Social Media Implications

- LinkedIn and YouTube posts should turn factory videos into small factory knowledge points.
- Example: drilling video should explain that different parts require different fixtures.
- Example: pump body sample videos can support pump body / pump housing project review content.
- Example: mold maintenance video can support production stability and batch consistency messaging.

### Customer Development Implications

- When reviewing target companies, HSX should not only ask whether they may need aluminum die casting.
- Also check whether their websites show application pages, technical product pages, supplier/RFQ pages, standards, housing/enclosure/pump/lighting product details, or signs of outsourced component needs.
- Deep-dive reviews should produce both buyer-fit judgement and website-learning points for HSX.

### Positioning Guardrails

- HSX is a real aluminum die casting factory.
- HSX focuses on small-to-medium aluminum die casting parts.
- CNC is only secondary machining after die casting when required.
- Do not position HSX as a CNC shop.
- Do not proactively mention 7075.
- Do not claim aerospace, military, brake, safety, load-bearing, performance, or structural functions unless specifically confirmed safe.
- Public content should focus on ordinary commercial and industrial aluminum die casting projects.
