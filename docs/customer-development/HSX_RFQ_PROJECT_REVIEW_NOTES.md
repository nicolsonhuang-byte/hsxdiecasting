# HSX RFQ and Project Review Notes

Internal document. This file records HSX DIECASTING real factory experience for RFQ review, project clarification, and customer communication. It may support future LinkedIn posts, website content, customer replies, and SOP drafting, but it should not expose customer-specific confidential details.

## 1. HSX RFQ Philosophy

HSX does not want to educate or blame customers. The goal is to solve problems efficiently based on the customer's actual requirements.

Clarifying key requirements early helps reduce rework, avoid unnecessary delays, reduce cost, and support smoother project delivery. Customer success reflects HSX's success.

HSX should not casually say "yes, we can do it" before understanding the key requirements, production risks, finishing expectations, and project constraints.

## 2. Common Missing RFQ Information

- No 3D file.
- Unclear product use or application.
- Unclear whether the part is decorative, housing-related, mounting-related, or load-bearing.
- Unclear surface finishing requirements.
- Unclear thermal conductivity or heat dissipation requirements, especially for lighting and electronic parts.
- For drilling and tapping, unclear hole position, number of holes, diameter, thread specification, drilling depth, tapping depth, tolerance, and whether the hole is blind or through.

## 3. Information That Affects Quotation and Project Judgment

- Production efficiency, evaluated case by case depending on product and mold design.
- Part weight.
- Material.
- Secondary processing.
- Hole positions and tapping details, including number, angle, size, and depth.
- Polishing requirements.
- Surface treatment requirements.
- Packaging.
- Quantity or annual volume.

## 4. Issues That Can Cause Rework or Delay

- Surface treatment not confirmed early, such as painting, powder coating, electroplating, anodizing feasibility, polishing, or other treatments.
- Future logo changes not considered before tooling.
- Material requirements unclear.
- Salt spray or corrosion testing requirements unclear.
- Load-bearing requirements or load level unclear.
- Final design, hole layout, assembly position, or logo area not confirmed before tooling.
- Unclear projects may need further confirmation before HSX proceeds, or HSX may decline if requirements remain undefined.

## 5. Small-to-Medium Aluminum Die Casting Review Points

- Product application and working environment.
- Annual quantity or expected order volume.
- Whether CNC machining is needed.
- Surface finishing route and feasibility.
- Special cleanliness or appearance requirements, such as CD texture, optical color effect under light, visible machining pattern, or high-cleanliness surface.
- Whether the final design is confirmed enough to avoid later mold modification.

## 6. Mold Design and Production Stability Experience

For heavier, thicker, or higher-volume aluminum die casting parts, production efficiency, mold temperature control, cooling design, and batch consistency may need to be considered during tooling review.

Early trial shots may look acceptable when the mold is not very hot, but during continuous production mold temperature can rise. If mold temperature, material temperature, injection speed, and injection force are not properly controlled, risks can include flash or burrs, shrinkage, air entrapment, incomplete filling, and surface issues.

These problems can increase later polishing difficulty and affect the final appearance. This should be described as project-dependent production-stability experience, not an absolute technical promise.

## 7. LED Light Housing RFQ Notes

- Clarify whether the lighting part is for indoor or outdoor use.
- Indoor lighting should distinguish normal indoor use from humid areas such as bathrooms.
- Bathroom lighting may need heat dissipation, waterproofing, and corrosion-resistance considerations.
- Outdoor lighting may need waterproofing, corrosion resistance, and salt spray testing.
- Factory installation and assembly efficiency should also be considered.
- Most lighting housing projects use powder coating.
- Anodizing or oxidation should only be discussed as project-dependent feasibility, not a default promise.
- High-temperature or thermal-demand applications may need heat dissipation or thermal conductivity review.

## 8. Photo/Sample-Only Inquiry Workflow

If a customer only provides photos and no 3D file, HSX usually asks for a physical sample. HSX can measure the sample and create 3D modeling or reverse engineering.

HSX can often infer product application by looking at the product, but special usage scenarios should be clarified. Material expectations should also be discussed, such as ordinary aluminum ingot, ADC12, national-standard or non-standard material, and whether future anodizing is being considered.

## 9. Logo, Inserts, and Hole Layout

- Logo changes are technically possible but should be confirmed early.
- Logo design may affect product thickness, quotation, insert design, mold modification risk, and mold service life.
- Letter height, marking depth, and logo complexity can affect the final quotation.
- Hole position changes can be more serious because they may affect structure, load-bearing, assembly, appearance, and final product function.
- Hole layout should be confirmed before tooling whenever possible.

## 10. Material and Standard Requirements

Customers should clarify whether there are industry standards, national standards, testing requirements, or regulatory requirements.

Customers should also clarify the product application and use environment. Material selection should be reviewed together with strength, corrosion resistance, heat dissipation, surface finishing, and long-term production requirements.

## 11. Confidentiality and Professional Ethics

- Customer-funded molds belong to the customer's project.
- HSX will not use customer-funded molds to supply unrelated customers.
- HSX will not provide customer-developed samples to unrelated customers.
- Customer drawings, dimensions, specifications, project parameters, mold details, and material or thermal requirements are confidential.
- General photos may sometimes be used only for safe capability display when appropriate, but details are not shared.
- If a customer says their project, photos, products, or samples cannot be displayed, HSX will not use them as display examples at all.
- Sensitive details are shared only internally when required for production.

## 12. Public Content Tone

Do not frame RFQ content as educating customers. Do not blame customers for missing information.

Use a collaborative tone:

> We clarify key details early to reduce rework, avoid unnecessary delays, and support smoother project delivery.

Public content may discuss general process and review points, but must not expose customer-specific details, material formulas, dimensions, drawings, tooling parameters, or thermal conductivity requirements.
