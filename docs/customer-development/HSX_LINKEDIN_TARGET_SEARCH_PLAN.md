# HSX LinkedIn Target Search Plan

Internal customer-development planning document for HSX DIECASTING.

Purpose: prepare a practical LinkedIn target customer search plan. Do not contact anyone from this plan unless separately instructed.

## 1. HSX Positioning for Target Search

HSX DIECASTING is a real aluminum die casting factory focused on:

- Small-to-medium aluminum die casting parts.
- OEM aluminum components.
- Die-cast aluminum LED light housing parts.
- Aluminum housings and enclosures.
- Pump body / pump housing aluminum die casting parts.
- Secondary machining after casting, such as drilling, tapping, and CNC finishing when required by the project.
- Surface finishing support, including polishing, powder coating, painting, and project-based finishing review.

HSX should not be positioned as a CNC machining shop, trading company, all-products supplier, EV giga casting supplier, or large structural die casting factory.

## 2. Ideal LinkedIn Target Customer Types

- LED fixture manufacturers with visible aluminum housings, flood lights, street lights, garden lights, high bay lights, or industrial lighting products.
- Outdoor and industrial lighting brands that may need aluminum light bodies, housings, brackets, heat-sink-like parts, or mounting components.
- Pump, water pump, fluid equipment, and small industrial equipment companies that may use die-cast aluminum pump bodies or housings.
- Aluminum housing, enclosure, hardware, and industrial component brands that sell physical products and may need OEM die-cast parts.
- OEM product development companies that help design or source physical industrial products.
- Industrial equipment brands with small-to-medium aluminum parts, covers, brackets, housings, or machined cast components.
- Importers or distributors with private-label physical products where aluminum housings or OEM metal components may be needed.
- Automotive accessory brands only for ordinary commercial aluminum parts, appearance parts, housings, brackets, or accessories without brake, safety, load-bearing, or performance claims.

## 3. Who Is Not a Good Target

- Pure retail stores with no OEM, sourcing, or product-development signal.
- Lighting installation companies, retrofit service providers, or project contractors with no manufacturing or product ownership.
- Solar/energy installation companies unless they clearly sell relevant physical products that may use aluminum housings.
- SMT/electronics-only companies without visible housing, enclosure, or mechanical component needs.
- Peer aluminum die casting factories, CNC shops, mold factories, or trading companies unless they are being observed for market research only.
- Companies focused only on light poles, steel structures, construction projects, or services rather than product manufacturing.
- C-end entertainment, car modification, racing, brake upgrade, or performance-oriented accounts.
- Companies that appear unrelated, inactive, unclear, or mostly content/marketing accounts without physical products.

## 5. Screening Rules

### A = Strong Potential Buyer

- Sells physical products that likely use aluminum die casting.
- Has visible LED fixtures, aluminum housings, pump bodies, enclosures, brackets, or industrial components.
- Mentions OEM, ODM, private label, product development, sourcing, engineering, purchasing, or manufacturing.
- Operates in relevant export markets such as the US, Canada, Europe, Middle East, Australia, or other B2B import markets.
- Appears to own or develop product lines rather than only install or resell services.

### B = Observe

- Has industrial, lighting, pump, enclosure, hardware, or product-development relevance, but aluminum die casting need is not clear yet.
- May be a distributor, importer, or product brand with possible future sourcing needs.
- May be a peer or larger manufacturer worth monitoring for market language, product trends, or buyer roles.
- Needs more evidence before any follow, connection, or outreach decision.

### C = Skip

- No visible physical product need.
- Service-only installer, contractor, consultant, or unrelated content account.
- Peer factory or supplier with no buyer signal.
- C-end retail, entertainment, racing, performance brake, or unsafe automotive positioning.
- No clear geography, business activity, product relevance, or contact-worthy role.

## 6. Manual Review Checklist

Before adding a company to any target list, check:

- Does the company sell physical products?
- Could their products use aluminum die casting?
- Do they seem like a buyer, not a peer factory?
- Do they mention OEM, ODM, product development, lighting, pump, housing, enclosure, hardware, or components?
- Do their products include LED lighting, aluminum housings, pump housings, industrial parts, brackets, covers, or enclosures?
- Do they operate in the US, Canada, Europe, Middle East, Australia, or another relevant export market?
- Is there a visible role worth observing later, such as founder, owner, sourcing manager, purchasing manager, product manager, engineering manager, R&D manager, or operations manager?
- Is the account active enough to show product direction or company priorities?
- Is there any sign that the company is only an installer, peer supplier, or unrelated retail account?

- Do not scrape personal data.

- Do not create ads.
