# HSX DIECASTING Public Asset Library

This document records how HSX DIECASTING should use publicly approved factory photos and videos in future website, SEO/GEO, LinkedIn, YouTube, and social media work.

## 1. Public Usage Status

The previously provided HSX factory photos and videos are approved for public use. They should still be described accurately, cautiously, and in a way that matches the actual process, part condition, and project stage shown in the asset.

Do not use these assets to imply unconfirmed final product performance, certifications, customer identity, commercial terms, or product applications that are not visible or confirmed.

## 2. Asset Categories

- Semi-finished OEM aluminum die casting parts
- Die-cast aluminum LED light housings / heat-sink-like parts
- Batch production of semi-finished aluminum die-cast parts
- In-house die casting mold maintenance
- Drilling, tapping, and secondary machining
- In-house polishing
- CNC-turned threaded aluminum component with vibratory finishing
- Practical finishing examples based on product application

## 3. Correct Public Descriptions

Use accurate wording such as:

- Semi-finished aluminum die casting parts
- In-process OEM aluminum die casting parts
- Parts before final machining, polishing, surface finishing, or assembly
- In-house die casting mold maintenance
- Cleaning residual material from venting areas
- CNC-turned threaded aluminum component with vibratory finishing
- Internal machined threaded component
- Die-cast aluminum LED light housing parts
- Small-to-medium OEM aluminum die casting parts

## 4. Wording to Avoid

- Do not describe semi-finished parts as finished final products.
- Do not describe the circular ribbed part as a large part unless size is confirmed.
- Do not describe internal threaded parts as decorative high-appearance products.
- Do not claim final performance, waterproof performance, braking performance, safety improvement, or certification unless confirmed by project agreement.
- Do not expose customer names, logos, confidential product identity, or commercial terms.

## 5. Suggested Use by Channel

### Website

- Use a few selected assets only after compression and review.
- Use factory process images to support OEM pages, light housing pages, project review pages, and future capability sections.

### LinkedIn

- Use process-focused posts for B2B buyers.
- Explain practical factory details, not only product display.

### YouTube

- Use short factory process videos such as mold maintenance, drilling/tapping, polishing, and semi-finished part inspection.
- Add website and RFQ links in descriptions.

### Social Media

- Use short clips with simple captions.
- Keep HSX positioned as a B2B factory.

## 7. Review Checklist Before Publishing

Before publishing each asset, check:

- Accurate description
- No misleading finished-product wording
- No customer logo or confidential marking
- No sensitive mold detail if avoidable
- No worker face if not needed
- Image/video cropped or compressed if needed
- Clear CTA to RFQ or website when used for B2B marketing
